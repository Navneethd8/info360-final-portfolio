Format
Instead of submitting a traditional paper, you will create a web portfolio that presents your design work.

You may use any platform you prefer, such as:

Google Sites
Notion
Wix
Figma prototype site
GitHub Pages
other website builders
Your site should be clear, visually organized, and easy to navigate.

Required Sections
Your web portfolio should include the following sections.

1. Problem
Explain the problem your design addresses and why it matters.

This section should clearly answer:

What problem exists in the world?
Who experiences this problem?
Why is the problem important?
Why do existing solutions fail to address it fully?
You may support your explanation using:

user research
empathy maps
interviews or observations
competitive analysis
scholarly research
literature review
Your goal is to convince the reader that the problem is real and meaningful.

 

2. Evaluation
Explain how your team evaluated the design.

You may include methods such as:

usability testing
cognitive walkthrough
heuristic evaluation
expert feedback
user feedback
Your evaluation should explain:

who participated
what tasks they performed
what worked well
what problems users experienced
what you learned from the evaluation
3. Design Rationale
One of the most important parts of this project is explaining why you made certain design decisions.

A strong design rationale answers questions such as:

Why did your team choose this solution?
Why is this design better than other ideas?
How did research influence your design?
What trade-offs did you consider?
Your reasoning may be supported by:

user research insights
usability testing
literature review
logical argumentation
The goal is to show that your design decisions were intentional and thoughtful.

 

4. Design Solution
Describe the design solution your team created.

This section should explain:

the core concept of your design
how users interact with the system
key features of the design
how the design addresses the problem
Include visual materials such as:

sketches
low-fidelity prototypes
high-fidelity prototypes
user flow diagrams
interface mockups
Your explanation should make it easy for someone to understand how the design works.

5. Reflection & Learning
This is the most important section of the final project.

Reflect on your design journey and learning experience.

Discuss:

what you learned about design thinking
challenges your team faced
how your ideas changed during the process
what you would improve if you had more time
how the project influenced your understanding of design
This section should demonstrate self-reflection and design maturity.

6. Limitations & Future Plan
No design is perfect. A strong designer understands the limitations of their work.

Discuss:

situations where your design may not work well
assumptions your design makes about users
trade-offs your team accepted
how the design could evolve in the future
This section demonstrates critical thinking and honesty about the design.

7. References
Include all sources used in your project, such as:

scholarly articles
books
websites
design resources
Use a consistent citation style.

Visual Expectations
Your web portfolio should include visual materials from your design process, such as:

prototypes
diagrams
screenshots
user flow diagrams
annotated mockups
Visuals should help explain your design decisions clearly.
